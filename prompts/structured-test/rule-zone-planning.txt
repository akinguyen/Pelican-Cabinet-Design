Rule file: Zone planning and work triangle

Use this file to decide how the kitchen should function.

Core zoning idea:
- The kitchen should be organized into meaningful zones instead of placing cabinets wall by wall with no workflow.

Important zones:
- Cooking
- Cold storage
- Wet-cleaning
- Prep-storage
- Pantry-storage
- Mixed
- Empty

Zone planning rules:
- Do not force zones to follow wall number order.
- Choose zones based on wall length, corners, fixed objects, appliance fit, workflow, visual balance, and storage needs.
- If a fixed object strongly implies a zone, respect it.
- Keep the refrigerator, sink/dishwasher, and range reasonably connected.
- Keep dishwasher directly beside sink when possible.
- Keep hood or microwave aligned with the cooking appliance when possible.
- If a zone idea creates blocked corners, unusable doors/drawers, or awkward appliance clearances, redesign the zone plan.
- If a wall works better as prep/storage than pantry/cold storage, use it creatively.

Work-triangle mindset:
- The refrigerator, sink, and cooking zone should usually feel connected.
- When feasible, aim for practical work-triangle legs in the general range of about 48 to 108 inches.
- If the room, catalog, or fixed objects prevent an ideal triangle, choose the best workable flow and explain the limitation in notes.
- Do not chase a perfect triangle if it breaks buildability, access, or corner correctness.

Appliance workflow reminders:
- Range/cooktop walls should feel visually balanced when possible.
- Refrigerator walls should avoid awkward narrow leftover cabinets.
- Sink/dishwasher zones should stay compact.
- Upper cabinets should align visually with the base/product layout when practical.

Order of planning:
1. Identify fixed-object constraints.
2. Choose likely zone walls.
3. Check whether corners can support those zone choices.
4. Adjust if needed before detailed cabinet placement.
