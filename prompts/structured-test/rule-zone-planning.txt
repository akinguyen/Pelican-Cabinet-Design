Rule file: Zone planning and work triangle

Use this after wall geometry is understood and before detailed placement.

Goal:
- Organize the kitchen into useful zones instead of filling walls randomly.

Allowed zone types:
- cooking
- cold-storage
- wet-cleaning
- prep-storage
- pantry-storage
- mixed
- empty

Zone rules:
- Choose zones from geometry, wall length, appliance fit, fixed objects, corners, workflow, and visual balance.
- Do not force zones to follow wall number order.
- Respect fixed objects that imply a zone.
- Redesign the zone plan if it creates blocked corners, unusable doors/drawers, appliance conflicts, or awkward access.

Workflow rules:
- Refrigerator, sink/dishwasher, and range/cooktop should feel connected.
- Keep the dishwasher directly beside the sink when possible.
- Keep the hood or microwave centered on the range/cooktop when possible.
- Aim for practical work-triangle legs around 48 to 108 inches when the room allows.
- Do not chase a perfect work triangle if it harms buildability, access, or corner correctness.

Appliance-zone reminders:
- Cooking zones should look balanced around the range/cooktop.
- Wet-cleaning zones should keep sink, dishwasher, trash, and prep space close.
- Cold-storage zones should avoid awkward narrow base cabinets beside refrigerators.
- Pantry-storage can use pantry cabinet stacks when space and access allow.
- Upper cabinets should align with base cabinets and products when practical.

Planning order:
1. Identify fixed-object constraints.
2. Choose likely zone walls.
3. Check whether corners support those zone choices.
4. Adjust zones before placing detailed cabinet runs.
