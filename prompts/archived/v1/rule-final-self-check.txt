Rule file: Final self-check

Use this checklist before returning the final JSON.

Geometry and wall checks:
- Kitchen shape/layoutType comes from actual wall geometry, not from labels alone.
- Wall structure is understood from `wallDots` and `wallShapeDotPairs`.
- Interior and exterior sides are identified correctly.
- Cabinet projection direction is correct for each chosen wall face.
- `leftInches` follows the chosen face start-to-end direction.
- Openings/gaps between non-connecting wall endpoints remain open.

Wall-use checks:
- Walls with `cabinetPlacementMode: "none"` have no placements.
- Every wall allowed for placement has at least one valid placement unless notes clearly explain why placement is impossible or invalid there.
- No allowed wall is accidentally ignored because the layout focused only on other walls.

Zone and workflow checks:
- Storage/cold, prep/washing, and cooking zones are established.
- Refrigerator, sink/dishwasher, and range/cooktop form a practical workflow when the room allows it.
- Work-triangle logic is respected when feasible without harming buildability.

Corner and blind checks:
- Inside corners are solved before the rest of the run is finalized.
- No normal cabinet/product is left blocked by a perpendicular run.
- Blind cabinets are used when needed at usable inside cabinet corners.
- Every blind cabinet has clear visible range, aligned/covered blind range, and no footprint overlap.
- No blind range is left visibly exposed.
- Perpendicular objects align with blind ranges by adjacency, not by overlap.
- If a blind range and perpendicular depth mismatch, the layout adjusts pull, built-in filler width, blind size, perpendicular depth, run position, or corner strategy.

Placement checks:
- All placements fit inside the selected wall-face span.
- No same-wall placements overlap.
- No floor-plan footprint overlap exists anywhere.
- No fixed object is moved, changed, or overlapped.
- End panels are used only for exposed visible sides.
- No filler and end panel are used for the same exact condition unless there is a real separate reason.

Output checks:
- JSON is valid.
- JSON matches the required output shape exactly.
- Notes briefly explain important constraints or design tradeoffs when needed.

Empty-space and refinement checks:
- After all placements are made, check each wall face separately for base-tier gaps and wall/upper-tier gaps.
- Confirm there is no empty usable gap that could accept a standard cabinet from the catalog.
- Confirm every standard-width gap, including 9, 12, 15, 18, 21, or 24 inches, has been tested against the catalog before leaving it empty.
- Confirm upper/wall cabinets are not forced to follow base-cabinet setbacks when the upper tier has a smaller valid corner setback and can accept another wall cabinet.
- Confirm any remaining empty space is smaller than the smallest usable cabinet option, is a required clearance/protected area, is an intentional opening, or is clearly explained in notes.

Adjacent filler self-check:
- Sort placements by wall, wallFace, tier, bottomInches, heightInches, and leftInches.
- Check whether any filler ends exactly where another filler begins on the same tier.
- If two fillers are adjacent, merge them into one filler unless a real non-filler object, fixed object, opening, or different vertical tier separates them.
- Confirm the final output has no pattern like `accessory-base-filler` followed immediately by `accessory-base-filler`, or `accessory-wall-filler` followed immediately by `accessory-wall-filler`, on the same wall face and tier.
- Scribe remainders should not be separate filler objects when they touch a required filler; they should be included in that required filler width.

Strict filler self-check:
- Every filler in the final JSON must have a valid reason: wall-end clearance, blind-corner clearance, or appliance clearance.
- Delete any filler whose only purpose is described as scribe, fit adjustment, center-span fit, decimal remainder, or alignment with another tier.
- Check that no filler is placed between two normal cabinets unless it is clearly required for appliance clearance or a real wall/fixed-boundary condition.
- Check that blind-corner fillers are exactly the intentional corner-clearance fillers; do not add separate tiny fillers beside them.
- Check that appliance fillers, when used on both sides of an appliance, look visually even whenever possible.
- Check that open run ends use end panels for exposed sides, not fillers.
- If a non-standard leftover space does not meet the three filler rules and cannot accept a standard cabinet, leave it unfilled and explain the limitation in notes instead of creating an invalid filler.
