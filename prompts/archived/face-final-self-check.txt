Rule file: Final self-check

Use this checklist before returning the final JSON.

Geometry and wall checks:
- Kitchen shape/layoutType comes from actual wall geometry, not from labels alone.
- Wall structure is understood from `wallDots` and `wallShapeDotPairs`.
- Interior and exterior sides are identified correctly.
- For each chosen wall face, `FACE-START` and `FACE-END` are resolved from the straight-on viewer perspective.
- The endpoint seen on the viewer's left is `FACE-START`; the endpoint seen on the viewer's right is `FACE-END`.
- `FACE-START` may be the wall's physical start or physical end depending on which wall face is projected.
- Cabinet projection direction is correct for each chosen wall face.
- `leftInches` is measured from `FACE-START` toward `FACE-END`, not automatically from wall-start toward wall-end.
- Openings/gaps between non-connecting wall endpoints remain open.

Wall-use checks:
- Walls with `cabinetPlacementMode: "none"` have no placements.
- Every wall allowed for placement has at least one valid placement unless notes clearly explain why placement is impossible or invalid there.
- No allowed wall is accidentally ignored because the layout focused only on other walls.

Zone and workflow checks:
- Storage/cold, prep/washing, and cooking zones are established.
- Refrigerator, sink/dishwasher, and range/cooktop form a practical workflow when the room allows it.
- Work-triangle logic is respected when feasible without harming buildability.

Corner and blind checks:
- Inside corners are solved before the rest of the run is finalized.
- No normal cabinet/product is left blocked by a perpendicular run.
- Blind cabinets are used when needed at usable inside cabinet corners.
- Every blind cabinet has clear visible range, aligned/covered blind range, and no footprint overlap.
- No blind range is left visibly exposed.
- Perpendicular objects align with blind ranges by adjacency, not by overlap.
- If a blind range and perpendicular depth mismatch, the layout adjusts pull, built-in filler width, blind size, perpendicular depth, run position, or corner strategy.

Placement checks:
- All placements fit inside the selected wall-face span.
- No same-wall placements overlap.
- No floor-plan footprint overlap exists anywhere.
- No fixed object is moved, changed, or overlapped.
- Fillers are used only for gaps or clearance.
- End panels are used only for exposed visible sides.
- No filler and end panel are used for the same exact condition unless there is a real separate reason.

Output checks:
- JSON is valid.
- JSON matches the required output shape exactly.
- Notes briefly explain important constraints or design tradeoffs when needed.
